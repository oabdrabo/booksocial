what it does

Your bookshelf is your profile.

EPUBs become readable posts, and reading becomes something you do with people. Every shot below is the real app — each next to what it does.

the feed

What your circle is reading

Books from people you follow, newest first — with likes, saves, threaded comments, and reposts with quotes. A continue-reading shelf picks every book back up where you left off, and a separate Explore tab surfaces everything public.

the reader

Read right in the app

A calm, distraction-free reading view with a chapter table of contents and your place saved automatically — so you always pick up where you stopped. Highlight the lines that land and keep private notes as you go.

profiles

A shelf that is your profile

Every profile is a bookshelf with the numbers that matter — followers, books shared, how much you've read, and a reading streak to keep you honest. Books without art get a handsome cover made from their title.

search

Search inside the books

Find the line you half-remember. Search looks inside the books themselves, not just titles, and jumps you straight to the passage with your words highlighted.

discover

Find your next read

An empty search box becomes discovery: popular books, people to follow, and a #tag cloud. Hashtags written in captions are indexed automatically and get their own tag pages.

book clubs

Read together

Spin up a book club around any book and chat with members in a shared space — create, join, or leave. Clubs are scoped to who can see the book.

messages

Direct messages

Slide into a private conversation about a book — with read receipts and unread badges. And when you block someone, the messages really do stop.

notifications

An activity inbox

Everything that happens to you in one place — likes, comments, replies, and @mentions — with unread indicators in the top bar.

privacy & control

Yours to lock down

Go private for followers-only visibility, block anyone (enforced across feeds, search, DMs and clubs), and edit your display name, bio and avatar.

why booksocial

Made for readers, not advertisers.

A reading club you actually own — no feeds gaming your attention, no one selling your taste.

Own your library

Your books, highlights, and notes live on your own server — not in someone else's cloud. No lock-in, ever.

Reading, together

Turn a solitary habit into something you share — follow friends, start a club, talk in the margins.

Private by default

Go followers-only, block whoever you like. Nothing is tracked, sold, or advertised.

Free, forever

Open source and free. No subscriptions, no ads, no upsells — just the app.

Runs on almost nothing

A tiny self-hosted app that's happy on a spare box or a Raspberry Pi at home.

Make it yours

The whole thing is open — change anything, host it your way, keep what you build.

get started

Up and reading in three commands.

Host it on your own machine — no accounts to sign up for, nothing to pay.

$ git clone https://github.com/oabdrabo/booksocial && cd booksocial
$ pip install -r requirements.txt && python seed.py
$ DEV_MODE=1 flask --app app run   # localhost:5000/?as=demo
